This post initiates an instance of the main theme outlined in our previous post. Namely, we begin by comparing cyclic and group cohomology. We have three applications in mind, which lead to very different types of mathematics. They are

- The cyclic cohomology of the mapping class group where we construct higher order signature cocycles for trisected four-manifolds.

- The moduli space of flat connections over a closed Riemann surface has a symplectic structure which can be described as a group cohomology class. We compare this with Witten's symplectic volume calculation and Reidemeister torsion.

- We extend these ideas to quandle cocycle invariants which lead us to consider quantum invariants. A comparison with quandle 3-cocycle invariants proves instructive.
